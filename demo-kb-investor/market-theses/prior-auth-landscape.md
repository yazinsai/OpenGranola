# Prior Authorization AI — Competitors and Market Map

Anyone who claims "no competition" or "nobody else is doing AI-powered prior auth" is wrong. This space has over $180M in total funding across at least 5 well-funded startups.

## Why This Space Is Hot

Prior auth costs US health systems ~$35B/year in administrative burden. Average prior auth takes 2 phone calls, 3 faxes, and 14 days. CMS finalized the Interoperability and Prior Authorization rule (CMS-0057-F) in January 2025, requiring payers to implement electronic prior auth APIs by January 2027. This regulatory tailwind is real.

## Funded Competitors

### Cohere Health
- **Raised:** $106M total (Series C, Polaris Partners)
- **What they do:** AI-powered prior auth and utilization management platform. Works with payers (insurance companies), not providers.
- **Traction:** Processes 50M+ prior auth decisions/year. Contracts with health plans covering 30M+ lives.
- **Moat:** Deep payer integrations, clinical guideline database, 5 years of decision data for model training.

### Olive AI (prior auth division → acquired by Waystar)
- **Raised:** $900M+ before imploding. Prior auth product line was the survivor.
- **What they do:** Automated prior auth submission and status tracking for health systems.
- **Status:** Waystar acquired the prior auth assets in 2024. Now integrated into Waystar's RCM platform.
- **Lesson:** Proves the market is real but also that horizontal "AI for everything in healthcare" doesn't work. Vertical focus wins.

### Infinitus Health
- **Raised:** $53M (Series B, a]6z)
- **What they do:** AI voice agents that make prior auth phone calls on behalf of providers. Literally calls insurance companies and waits on hold.
- **Traction:** Handles 500K+ calls/month. Partners with major health systems.
- **Moat:** Proprietary phone conversation AI trained on millions of payer interactions. Hard to replicate.

### Rhyme Health
- **Raised:** $12M (Seed, General Catalyst)
- **What they do:** Prior auth automation focused on specialty medications. Integrates with EHRs to auto-fill auth forms.
- **Traction:** Early — ~20 specialty pharmacy partners.
- **Differentiator:** Narrow focus on specialty pharma where prior auth denial rates are 30%+ and each denial costs $2K+ in rework.

### Valer Health
- **Raised:** $8.5M (Seed)
- **What they do:** Real-time prior auth determination at the point of care. Doctor orders a procedure, Valer instantly predicts whether it'll be approved.
- **Traction:** Pilot with 3 health systems.

## Key Questions for Any New Entrant

1. Are you selling to payers or providers? Completely different sales motion and buyer.
2. What's your clinical data advantage? Cohere has 5 years of decision data. What do you have?
3. How do you handle the long tail of payer-specific rules? Each insurance company has different criteria for every procedure code.
4. CMS mandate is a tailwind but also means Epic/Cerner will build basic prior auth automation. How do you stay ahead of the EHR vendors?
5. Infinitus proved you can automate the phone calls. Are you competing with them or building on top of the API pathway?
